# 📊 Superstore Sales Analysis — Excel Mini Project

A mini project analysing 2,538 US retail orders (2014–2017) across 20 states using 20 Excel formulas — from raw data to a final business report.

**Live Sheet:** [Google Sheets — Mini Project](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1wLzTCdNmkfv2EL10ZBqUDRmVZvS_7ajmmTona14kckA/edit?usp=sharing)
**Author:** Akshat Dixit · [GitHub: AkshatDweb](https://github.com/AkshatDweb)

## Dataset
- 2,538 order records · 20 fields · 764 customers
- 48 US states · 3 categories · 17 sub-categories
- Fields: Order ID/date, Ship Mode, Customer, Segment, State, Region, Category, Sales, Quantity, Discount, Profit

## What's Inside
20 questions solved per state (Q1–Q20) using:
`INDEX/MATCH` · `COUNTIF` · `SUMIF/SUMIFS` · `MINIFS/MAXIFS` · `RANK` · date functions · nested `IF` · conditional formatting

Covers: region lookup, order/sales totals, state ranking & contribution %, segment-wise sales, first/last order dates, profit after 18% tax, and profit-slab classification.

## Key Results
- **Total sales:** $438.1K across 20 states | **Profit:** $37.5K before tax, $30.8K after
- **California & New York** drive ~39% of sales and 84% of profit
- **Consumer segment** leads in 16/20 states (52.8% of sales)
- 6 states lose money — mainly due to heavy discounting (Illinois, Texas, Pennsylvania)

## Recommendations
1. Protect top states (CA, NY)
2. Fix discount-driven losses (IL, TX, PA)
3. Grow the Consumer segment further
4. Replicate Michigan's low-discount, high-profit model
